Trust psychology — what works for legal tech

Trust in legal/financial interfaces is driven by competence cues (structure, navy/ink blue, serif document typography), familiarity (paper-like surfaces evoke real statutes and filings), and restraint (no neon, no gamification, no false urgency). Research on color psychology consistently ranks blue highest for institutional trust; warm cream/yellow paper tones reduce anxiety and signal "document" rather than "marketing." Green works only as a sparse "verified" signal — not as a primary brand color.

Best overall for Dharmiq

Ashoka Clarity or Archival Bond — combine institutional competence (navy/ink) with paper warmth. Ashoka for civic authority; Archival for "this is a real legal document."

Paper + yellow family

Cream and manila tones trigger document authenticity and lower cortisol vs stark white. Keep yellow muted (never bright #FFD700) — bright yellow reads as warning/caution in UI.

What to avoid

Purple AI gradients, saturated primaries, dark-default for citizens, rounded-playful without structure, animation that fakes progress. All reduce perceived legal credibility.

Hybrid recommendation

Archival Bond palette + Ashoka's verified-citation pattern + Village Square's vernacular typography fallback. Paper bg, ink blue citations, green only on "source verified."
